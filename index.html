The World of Heldidon
Hel · di · don — the home world

Heldidon is the seat of the known world — a land of ancient curses and fiery coastlines, of whispering forests older than memory and desert oases glittering with impossible crystals. It is a world shaped equally by those who build and those who burn, where adventurers rise from tavern doors and vanish into legend.

"Welcome — let's start prep. This is the wiki for the TTRPG world of Heldidon, now running on the Daggerheart system."

From the coastal city of Embermere to the cursed mists of Ashenford, from the hedge-walls of the Rootbound Labyrinth to the black waters of the Mistweave River — Heldidon rewards those who explore it and punishes those who don't prepare. Five towns serve as anchors of civilization. Seven natural landmarks hide danger and treasure alike. Four connected realms — Terravolca, Sylvarien, Codexia, and the dreaded Vethara — bleed through at the edges. One of them only releases you when you are ready.

Factions of Heldidon
Six powers shaping the world from the shadows and the light
🔴
Red Requiem
Assassin Guild · Ruthless · Infamous

Merciless killers and assassins, operating from the shadows. Leader: Madam Red, based at Lexorith Manor. Second: Lucy.

Rose Coins: Their own currency stamped with a rose — banned in Embermere, used for black market trades. Rumoured to be forged from the melted jewellery of their victims, and cursed to mark those who spend them too freely.

Silent Oaths: Members adopt crimson titles — Scarlet Fang, Crimson Veil, Vermilion Shade — and never speak their true names.

Rumour: Every rose coin carries a death mark for those who spend them too freely.
🧭
The Forgotten Compass
Arcane Society · Neutral · Mysterious

Seekers of lost knowledge and ancient magic. Leader: Andrew Rivers. Present in Embermere and Scriptoria.

Codexia Doors: Hidden portals to Codexia disguised as cracked mirrors, weathered archways, and forgotten wells. Only Keepers know the sigils.

Vetharan Banishment: The Forgotten Compass are the only faction who possess the spell to send someone into Vethara. It is never written down — memorised only by senior Keepers and passed verbally. Crucially, the spell only works one way. There is no return spell. Once inside, even a Keeper must earn their own way out through Varek's judgement.

Rumour: The Forgotten Compass can predict calamities before they happen.
🏮
The Golden Lantern
Adventuring Guild · Warm · Protective

The general adventuring guild found across Heldidon. Run by retired adventurers called Managers — no single leader. Present at Thornspire Hub, Embermere (Gilded Net), Ashenford (The Last Drink), and Scriptoria (Golden Dove).

Lantern Symbolism: Every hall bears an enchanted golden lantern that never extinguishes — guidance, safety, and light in the dark.

Rumour: Some of the greatest heroes in Heldidon started their careers at Thornspire.
🤫
The Silent Honor
Holy Order · Lawful · Feared

Silent warriors and paladins bound by a sacred vow. Present in Embermere. Exist as scattered clans — some vow total muteness, others only ritual silence.

Symbols: Roaring beast tattoos (lion, wolf, dragon) carved into armor — the roar of their deeds.

"They speak not, but their deeds roar."

Words are fleeting, but actions carve eternity.
🐍
The Dune Serpents
Thieves' Guild · Criminal · Desert-Based

A thieves' guild controlling smuggling routes and black-market trade, based in Zerathis. Guildmaster: The Serpent's Tongue — identity concealed even from most members.

Hierarchy: The Fang (assassins) → The Coil (smugglers) → The Scale (thieves & informants).

Rivals: Clash with the Golden Lantern (disrupts operations) and Red Requiem (compete for contracts).

Rumour: They use snake venom as ink to sign black market contracts.
🔥
Order of the Crystal Flame
Religious Guild · Devout · Desert-Bound

A devout religious order centred on the colossal crystal spire in Zerathis. Priests, mystics, and flamebearers who oversee rituals and guard the sanctity of the spire.

Belief: The spire is a divine beacon channelling celestial power into the desert and guiding both pilgrims and rulers.

The visions flamebearers receive are said to be instructions — not prophecy.

Crafting Guide
How to brew potions in the world of Heldidon
The Brewing Process
1
Gather Ingredients
Ingredients must be harvested from their source location in Heldidon. Most require a successful Instinct or Knowledge roll (Difficulty set by rarity: Common = 10, Uncommon = 13, Rare = 16, Legendary = 20) to find and collect without damaging them.
2
Find a Brewing Space
Potions require at least a small cauldron, a heat source, and clean water. Common and Uncommon potions can be brewed at any campfire. Rare potions need an alchemist's lab or the Grimwater Crossing. Legendary potions require specific sacred sites.
3
Combine & Brew
Make a Knowledge roll using the Potion's Difficulty. On Hope: perfect brew, full effect. On Fear: brew succeeds but a minor complication occurs (odd colour, strange taste, short duration). On failure: ingredients are lost and the brew is ruined.
4
Bottle & Store
Potions last indefinitely if stored in sealed glass vials. You may carry up to 5 potions at once. Potions stored loose (unstoppered) lose potency after 3 days. Legendary potions must be consumed within 24 hours of brewing.

Experimental Combinations

When players combine ingredients not listed in known recipes, the GM may allow an experimental brew. Roll Knowledge (Difficulty 15). On a Hope result, create a new potion with a surprising beneficial effect drawn from both ingredients' raw properties. On a Fear result, the brew has an unexpected side effect alongside its benefit. On a failure, the ingredients are destroyed and something unpleasant happens.

💡 Experiment Idea
Grimwater Essence + Moonflower Petals: The coven's dark water and the clarity flower — possibly creates a truth serum, or a potion that lets you see a creature's memories.
💡 Experiment Idea
Phoenix Ashes + Grimwater Essence: The heat of rebirth and the cold of the deep river — possibly creates a potion of temporary invulnerability or a wildly unstable explosive.
💡 Experiment Idea
Starlight Dust + Mothwing Powder: Stars and nocturnal moths — possibly creates a potion of dreamwalking or astral projection for one scene.
